Glaciers, Whales & Wildlife

Seward Harbor → Kenai Fjords Cruise → Exit Glacier
🚢
Morning (departs ~8–9 AM)
Kenai Fjords Full-Day Glacier Cruise
This is the highlight of the Kenai Peninsula and honestly one of the best days you'll have in Alaska. An 8-hour boat tour takes you out of Resurrection Bay into the open Gulf of Alaska. You'll see calving tidewater glaciers up close, puffins, sea otters, sea lions, Steller's sea lions, and in July — very likely humpback whales and possibly orcas.
Book with Kenai Fjords Tours (the most established operator). Dress in layers — it's cold on the water even in July. The boat has a heated cabin and hot chowder on board.
🧊
Evening (after cruise, ~6 PM)
Exit Glacier — Walk to the Ice
Just 9 miles from Seward. Walk the easy outwash plain trail right up to the glacier's edge. Signs along the path mark where the glacier stood in 1926, 1951, 1980, 2000 — you can physically see how far it has retreated. It's stunning and a little emotional.
The walk to the base is flat and easy — about 30 min round trip. Wear layers, the glacier makes its own cold wind.
🍽️
Evening · July 4th Fireworks!
Dinner & 4th of July in Seward
You'll be back from the cruise by 7–8 PM. Grab dinner on 4th Ave and stay out for the fireworks — Seward does a big 4th of July celebration over the harbor. Perfect end to an epic day.

Monday, July 6  ·  The Big One

Denali National Park

Talkeetna → Denali Entrance → Deep Wilderness Bus Tour
🚌
Check out by 11 AM · Then drive 2 hrs to park
Tundra Wilderness Tour Bus (8 hrs)
Private cars can only go to Mile 15 in Denali. Beyond that, you ride the park's own bus deep into the wilderness. The Tundra Wilderness Tour goes to Mile 53 — that's where the real wildlife is. A park naturalist is on board, narrates the whole route, and stops for every grizzly, caribou, Dall sheep, and moose sighting. On a clear day, Denali's full summit is visible.
Book at reservedenali.com — this is the #1 thing to book before your trip. Bring snacks, layers, and a camera with zoom. The bus has no WiFi.
🐻
During the tour
What you'll likely see
July is peak wildlife season in Denali. Grizzly bears with cubs are common. Caribou herds cross the road. Dall sheep dot the high ridges. Moose wade in the ponds. And on a clear day — Denali, the tallest peak in North America at 20,310 ft, fills the entire sky.
🏕️
Evening
Stay near the park entrance
There are several lodges and cabins clustered right outside the park entrance on the Parks Highway. Check in, have dinner, and enjoy the long July daylight — it won't be fully dark until nearly midnight.

Denali Morning & Drive Back

Denali → Anchorage (~4.5 hrs) → Fly home Jul 8
🥾
Early Morning
Morning Hike — Savage River Loop or Horseshoe Lake
Both trails are easy, beautiful, and accessible without a bus. The Savage River Loop (2 miles) has incredible mountain views. Horseshoe Lake Trail is flat and peaceful through spruce forest with lake reflections. Either way, mornings are magical here — misty, quiet, and golden.
🐕
~10 AM
Free Sled Dog Demonstration at the Kennels
Denali is the only national park with a working sled dog team. They maintain the dogs year-round for winter patrol operations. The daily demo is free, genuinely wonderful, and about 30 minutes. Rangers bring out the dogs and explain how the teams work in winter. Don't skip this.
🚗
Head back south on Parks Highway to Anchorage. ~4.5 hours. The Alaska Range is visible on your left the whole way.
✈️

Good to Know

Tips for Sophie & Rashed

Things that'll make the trip smoother
👕

Layer Everything

July in Alaska means 60–70°F in Anchorage, but 40s on the glacier cruise and in Denali. Bring a light puffy jacket, a rain shell, and a fleece. Even in summer, evenings get cold fast.

⛽

Fill Up Gas Often

Gas stations are sparse outside Anchorage. Fill up whenever you see one — Seward, Talkeetna, and near Denali all have limited options and higher prices.

🌞

Sun Until Midnight

In July, Alaska barely gets dark. Bring an eye mask for sleeping, or you'll be wide awake at 11 PM wondering why it still looks like 6 PM outside.

📶

Expect No Signal

Cell service disappears outside Anchorage and Seward. Download offline maps (Google Maps lets you save areas). The Denali bus has no WiFi — bring downloaded music or podcasts.

🐻

Wildlife From a Distance

You'll see bears and moose — that's the point! Stay at least 300 ft from bears and 25 ft from moose at all times. On the Denali bus, the driver handles all wildlife stops safely.

💊

Sea Sickness on Day 2

The Kenai Fjords cruise goes into the open Gulf of Alaska, which can be rough. If either of you gets seasick, take Dramamine the night before and the morning of the cruise.

🎒

What to Pack in Your Day Bag

Water bottle, snacks for the bus, sunscreen (the sun is intense at altitude), lip balm, and a small camera or phone mount for the cruise railing.

💰

Budget Snapshot

Kenai Fjords cruise: ~$200–250/person. Denali bus: ~$70/person. Rental car: ~$500–700/week. Hotels: ~$150–250/night. Budget $3,500–5,000 total for two people (excl. flights).
